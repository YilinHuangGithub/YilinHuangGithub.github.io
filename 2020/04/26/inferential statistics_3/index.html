Inferential Statistics 3 - Hypothesis Testing

Author: Yilin Date: April 26, 2020  13:45:33 Category: Statistics

1. Introduction

Statistical hypothesis testing assumes that hypothesis that is testable on the basis of observing a process that is modeled via a set of random variables. Commonly, two statistical data sets are compared. Two hypotheses will be involved in the testing which are null hypothesis and alternative hypothesis. It will be determined by threshold for the statistical probability whether the null hypothesis will be rejected and to accept the alternative hypothesis.

Four classical hypothesis testing will be introduced which are T test, Z test, F test and Chi-square test.

3. Why we need hypothesis testing ‚Äì problems solved by hypothesis testing

In the real world, we want to evaluate but it is very hard to get all the data for the general population. But we could collect and utilize samples from this population to infer the general population.

For example, we have two versions for an APP and we want to know that which version will have higher conversion rate. We could conduct an A/B testing experiment to collect sample from APP for a period of time. From this experiment result generates from these sample, we know version A have the better performance. Based on the hypothesis testing, we could assume that the conversion rate for this APP will show the same result which is version A have the better conversion rate than version B in the future of APP deployment.

4. The process of hypothesis testing

‚Ä¢ State null hypothesis and alternative hypothesis.

‚Ä¢ Specify which Œ± level (significance level) according to the equation setting for null and alternative hypothesis. Usually which between 1% and 5%.

‚Ä¢ Collect sample data.

‚Ä¢ Compute the P-value based on Œ± level.

‚Ä¢ Draw a conclusion about H0 based on P value.

5. What you should know about the hypothesis testing

Null hypothesis and alternative hypothesis

For the hypothesis testing, there will have two type hypothesis which are null hypothesis and alternative hypothesis.

Usually, we want to get the P value lowers than the significant level or Œ± level to reject the Null hypothesis and accept the alternative hypothesis.

The equation setting for the Null hypothesis usually will be =, >=, <=.
The equation setting for the Alternative hypothesis usually will be !=, <=, >=.

Single and two population hypothesis testing

For the hypothesis testing, we want to figure out the problems by using data for one population or two different population. For example, we want to figure out the conversion rate for an App in two period and we did not separate the users into different group. The population for this experiment is single hypothesis testing. Another example for two population hypothesis testing is that we separate user into two group for different version of App and conduct testing to figure out whether the mean value for these two group is significant different.

The assumptions for these two hypotheses are:

‚Ä¢ The random values are all independent and not influence each other.

‚Ä¢ No outlier in the sample data.

‚Ä¢ Close to normal distribution.

But two sample t test requires variances of the two distributions are the same which is quoted as homogeneity of variance or homoscedasticity.

Definition: The result of significant level is given you a probability which is denoted by
Œ± of the study to reject the null hypothesis give the null hypothesis is to be true. According to the Œ±, you could determine by the P value whether or not the mean value for two sample are statistically significant different.

The difference between significance level, confidence level and confidence interval

Significance level: In a hypothesis test, the significance level, alpha, is the probability of making the wrong decision when the null hypothesis is true.

Confidence level: The probability that if a poll/test/survey were repeated over and over again, the results obtained would be the same. A confidence level = 1 - alpha.

Confidence interval: A range of results from a poll, experiment, or survey that would be expected to contain the population parameter of interest. For example, an average response. Confidence intervals are constructed using significance levels / confidence levels.

P value

Definition: P-value or probability value is the probability of obtaining test results at least as extreme as the results actually observed during the test, assuming that the null hypothesis is correct.

6. Classical Hypothesis Testing

T-Test

Why we need T-test?

If you want to check whether or not two means from a population or two population are significant different between each other. You could apply T-test to infer from sample data to the general population since it is difficult to collect the data from the population.

Definition

T-test is a statistical hypothesis test in which test statistic follow student‚Äôs t-distribution under the null hypothesis. The t-test can be used to determine if the means of two sets of data are significant different from each other.

Assumptions

‚Ä¢ The means of the two populations being compared should follow normal distributions.

‚Ä¢ The two populations being compared should have the same variance.

‚Ä¢ The data used to carry out the test should either be sampled independently from the two populations being compared or be fully paired.

Distribution

The distribution for t-test is t distribution. t-distribution is a member of continuous probability distributions family that arises when estimating the mean of a normally distributed population in situations where the sample size is small with degree of freedom and the population standard deviation is unknown.

For t-test, the T-distribution is to assess the statistical significance of the difference between two sample mean. And t-distribution also arises from Bayesian analysis of data from a normal family.

If you want to more detail, you could check out my another post about statistical distributions.

Alternative hypothesis usually will be Group A != Group B, Group A <= Group B, Group A >= Group B.

Z Test

Why we need Z-test?

Z-test is formed from T-test. It would apply when the test statistics would follow a standard normal distribution. Unlike T-test, the Z-test calculate when the deviation for the population has already known.

Definition

A Z-test is any statistical test for which the distribution of the test statistic under the null hypothesis can be approximated by a normal distribution.

Assumptions

‚Ä¢ Distributions of the two populations are normal.

‚Ä¢ The variances of the two distributions are known and are the same.

‚Ä¢ we already know the population variance œÉ (Because of the central limit theorem, many test statistics are approximately normally distributed for large samples).

‚Ä¢ sample data larger than 30.

Distribution

Z-distribution (standard normal distribution) The standard normal distribution is centered at zero and the degree to which a given measurement deviates from the mean is given by the standard deviation.

You could learn more details about Z-distribution from my another post statistical distribution.

Type

The term ‚ÄúZ-test‚Äù is often used to refer specifically to the one-sample location test comparing the mean of a set of measurements to a given constant when the sample variance is known.

Hypotheses

The hypotheses depend on the assumption that you want to test for this experiment.

Null hypothesis usually will be Group A = Group B, Group A >= Group B, Group A <= Group B.

Alternative hypothesis usually will be Group A != Group B, Group A <= Group B, Group A >= Group B.

Chi-square Test

Why we need Chi-square test?

Unlike T-test and Z-test for continuous data, Chi-square applys to the categorical data to test whether there is statistically significant difference between the variance for the observed frequencies and expected frequencies.

Definition

Chi-square test is a statistical hypothesis test that is valid to perform when the test statistic is chi-squared distributed under the null hypothesis. It is used to determine whether there is a statistical difference between the expected frequencies and the observed frequencies in on or more categories of a so-called contingency table.

Assumptions

‚Ä¢ The observations are independent and normally distributed.

Distribution

The chi-square distribution is used in the common chi-square tests for goodness of fit of an observed distribution to a theoretical one; The independence of two criteria of classification of qualitative data; And in confidence interval estimation for a population standard deviation of a normal distribution from a sample standard deviation.

You could learn more details about chi-square distribution from my another post statistical distribution.

Hypotheses

Null hypothesis assumes that Variable A and Variable B are independent.

Alternative hypothesis assumes that Variable A and Variable B are not independent.

Types

Chi-square test likes T-test has one sample test and two sample test. And for two sample test includes independent sample and paired sample. If the variable has more than 2 classification, we need to apply different method in two-sample chi-square test.

F-Test

Why we need F-test?

Unlike T and Z test only for 2 samples, F-test could apply to multiple groups of data (2 or more than 2 groups). F-test is usually applied to check that whether there is statistically significant difference between this group by their variance.

Definition

An F-test is conducted by the researcher on the basis of the F statistic. The F statistic is defined as the ratio between the two independent chi square variates that are divided by their respective degree of freedom.

The F-test is designed to test if multiple population variances are equal to identify whether these populations are come from the same population. It does this by comparing the ratio of multiple variances. So, if the variances are equal, the ratio of the variances will be 1. Another application of Exact ‚ÄúF-tests‚Äù mainly arise when the models have been fitted to the data using least squares for linear regression.

Assumptions

‚Ä¢ The populations from which the samples were obtained must be normal.

‚Ä¢ The samples must be independent

7. Differences between hypothesis testing

Differences between T test and Z test.

‚Ä¢ T-test have the degree of freedom to calculate t value and without knowing the variance. Usually the sample size is smaller 30.

‚Ä¢ Z-test with larger sample larger than 30 and with the population variance have already known.

Differences between T test (Z test) and F test.

‚Ä¢ T and Z tests apply to only two group of sample and check their mean value whether or nor have the statistically significant difference.

‚Ä¢ F test applys to multiple groups (2 or more than 2 group) and check their variance whether or not come from same population.

Differences between T test (Z test) and Chi-square test.

‚Ä¢ T and Z tests for continuous data to check their mean value.

‚Ä¢ Chi-square test for categorical data to check the observed frequencies and expected frequencies.

8. Summary

Hope this post will help you get a clue about hypothesis testing. Basically the hypothesis testing is to infer the conclusion to the general population from the samples based on the statistical assumption. Because it is difficult to collect all the data from the general population. In general, the assumption for the hypotheis testing requires the sample to be independent and normal distribution and then you could do the hypothesis testing. For different hypothesis testing, the purpose and the data type could apply to the hypothesis testing are different. Before you conduct the hypothesis, it is important for you to understand very detail about the problem you want to solve firstly and what types of data you have.

Excep these four classical hypothesis testing which are T-test, Z-test, Chi-square test and F-test, there are also others hypothesis testing to solve different problem. However if you learn these four hypothesis testing well, it will be very easy for to understand others.
