Author: Yilin Date: April 26, 2020  13:51:55 Category: Case Study

Introduction

Case study is important if you want to find a job in the consulting firms. Although they do not look for the candidates from a very specific industry, they require candidate should have an excellent problem-solving ability which could be evaluated by the case study.

This post will introduce about what is the case study and how to prepare for that.

Case Interview Definition

A case interview is a job interview in which the applicant is presented with a challenging business scenario that he/she must investigate and propose a solution to. Case interviews are designed to test the candidate’s analytical skills and ‘soft’ skills within a realistic business context. The case is often a business situation or a business case that the interviewer has worked on in real life.

Skills will be evaluated from case study

During the case interviews, interviews are generally looking for the following skills:

• Numerical and verbal reasoning skills.

• Communication and presentation skills.

• Business skills and commercial awareness.

Suggested preparing process for case interview

  1. Find and organize resources of case study online such as books, video, audio and case study clubs.

  2. Learning the frameworks about different scenarios.

  3. Find and practice with your partners.

  4. Summarize the cases you have practices.

After practice and summarize around 10 cases, you will find that case study is pretty simple and basically focus on the same factors such as Company, Customers, Competitors and Market. What you need to practice more is you need to think flexible and as more as you can when you do the cases in different industries or scenerios.

The process of case interview:

  1. Interviewer delivers the context/background of a case.

  2. Candidate repeats the information concisely and double checks whether or not this case has a very specific goal what to achieve. For example, to make 50% more profit in the profit decrease scenario.

  3. Candidate asks for a few moments to organize your thought based on the background of case. (no more than 30 seconds)

3.Candidate presents your thought concisely and structure about this case. (MECE principle from Mckinsey will be very helpful if you want to know how do it well in this part)

4.Interviewer may ask questions for this case or ask you that which part about your structure that you want to start firstly.
It depends on what kind of type for this case which may interviewer lead or interviewee lead case.
